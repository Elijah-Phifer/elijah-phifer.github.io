Elijah Phifer

I am a computer science undergraduate with a strong interest in pursuing a PhD. My interests are in uncertainty, decision-making, and Bayesian statistics. I explore areas like probabilistic programming, dynamic game theory, and multi-agent systems, with a particular interest in applying these concepts to robotics and simulation. I am passionate about developing statistical models and leveraging probabilistic algorithms to solve complex problems in uncertain environments. I also like to think about epistemic and aleatory uncertainty, and how these concepts relate not only to science but to the broader human experience.

  • Statistical Inference

  • Game Theory (Bayesian Games, Stochastic Games)

  • Cooperative and Non-cooperative Robots
